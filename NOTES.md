Our best configuration features a 5-layer, 6-head GPT model with a hidden size of 144 and tied embedding/softmax weights, achieving exactly 1.88M parameters to maximize the 2M cap. We replaced the raw byte tokenizer with a BPE tokenizer (vocab size 4096), compressing the text sequence length by 3.5x, which drastically improves contextual understanding within the constraints, especially for Devanagari text. The block size was increased to 256. For optimization, we used AdamW with a weight decay of 0.1 and Cosine Annealing with a 10% linear warmup. We implemented gradient accumulation (2 steps with batch size 16) to reach an effective batch size of 32, allowing the model to train over roughly two full epochs of the dataset in just 2,000 steps. Gradient clipping at norm 1.0 prevented early destabilization. Finally, we introduced proper GPT-2 style initialization by scaling residual projections down by `1/sqrt(2 * n_layer)`. This combination allowed rapid, stable convergence within the tight time and step limits.
